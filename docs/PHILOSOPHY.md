# Philosophy

Using AI is like using a table saw. You can cut off your hand or build something beautiful. The difference comes down to experience, tools, and custom jigs.

A jig is accumulated knowledge made physical — a previous decision you don't have to remake every cut. You build it once from hard-won experience, and from then on it gives you repeatable precision without improvisation. In AI, that's your prompts, workflows, system instructions, and agent configs: the fixtures that encode what you've learned so you're not starting from scratch every time.

But the saw doesn't make you a better woodworker automatically, and it doesn't know what you're building. That clarity has to come from you. The quality of what you make is bounded by the sharpness of your intent.

Same with AI.
